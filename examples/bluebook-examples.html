Bluebook Citation Examples

Working Citation Cookbook for the GTLS Quarto Legal Template

Author

Robert C. Cline, CPL

Published

July 2, 2026

Purpose

This document is a working citation cookbook for the GTLS Quarto Legal Template. It is intended to collect tested examples of legal, technical, governmental, and data-source citations that render correctly through Quarto, Pandoc, and the project Bluebook CSL style.

Use this file as a reference when writing research papers in the series Research Papers on Land, Water, and Mineral Law.

Important Quarto Citation Syntax

Standard citation

Use a normal Pandoc citation when the authority is not named in the sentence:

Nebraska regulates groundwater through statute [@Neb46_701].

Example output:

Nebraska regulates groundwater through statute (Nebraska Legislature 2025a).

Suppress author or authority name

Use [-@key] when the authority is already named in the sentence:

Nebraska regulates groundwater through the *Nebraska Ground Water Management and Protection Act* [-@Neb46_701].

Example output:

Nebraska regulates groundwater through the Nebraska Ground Water Management and Protection Act (2025a).

Multiple citations

Separate multiple citations with semicolons:

Nebraska groundwater regulation involves both statutory authority and local NRD administration [@Neb46_701; @Neb46_739_02].

Example output:

Nebraska groundwater regulation involves both statutory authority and local NRD administration (Nebraska Legislature 2025a, 2025b).

Pinpoint citation

For page or section references, add the locator after the key:

A secondary source may be cited to a specific page [@GroundwaterTransfersNebraska, p. 12].

Example output:

A secondary source may be cited to a specific page (GroundwaterTransfersNebraska?).

Nebraska Authorities

Nebraska Statute — Act or Statutory Scheme

Use this form for a statutory act or range of sections.

@misc{Neb46_701,
  author       = {{Nebraska Legislature}},
  title        = {Nebraska Ground Water Management and Protection Act},
  howpublished = {Neb. Rev. Stat. §§ 46-701 to 46-756},
  year         = {2025},
  note         = {Current through the 2025 legislative session}
}

Working citation:

Nebraska regulates groundwater through the Nebraska Ground Water Management and Protection Act (2025a).

Nebraska Statute — Specific Section

Use this form for a specific Nebraska Revised Statute section.

@misc{Neb46_739_02,
  author       = {{Nebraska Legislature}},
  title        = {Recording Transfer of Right to Use Ground Water},
  howpublished = {Neb. Rev. Stat. § 46-739.02},
  year         = {2025},
  note         = {Current through the 2025 legislative session}
}

Working citation:

Nebraska law provides for recording an approved transfer of the right to use groundwater (2025b).

Nebraska Administrative Rule

Use this form for NRD rules and regulations. Replace the example with the district, chapter, rule, and current date.

@misc{ExampleNRDRule,
  author       = {{Example Natural Resources District}},
  title        = {Groundwater Management Rules and Regulations},
  howpublished = {Example NRD Rules ch. 5, § 3},
  year         = {2025},
  note         = {Example placeholder; replace with actual NRD rule citation}
}

Working citation:

NRD transfer procedures are often governed by district-specific groundwater rules (Example Natural Resources District 2025b).

Secondary Authorities

Law Review Article

@article{LawReviewExample,
  author  = {Doe, Jane},
  title   = {Groundwater Transfers and Local Water Governance},
  journal = {Nebraska Law Review},
  volume  = {100},
  number  = {1},
  pages   = {1--45},
  year    = {2025}
}

Working citation:

Secondary legal commentary can help frame statutory and administrative analysis (Doe 2025).
