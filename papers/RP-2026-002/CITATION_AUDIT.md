# Citation Audit — Nebraska Groundwater Transfer Article

**Purpose.** You asked that citations contain only actual laws and sources, with no
hallucinations. This log records how each authority was verified during drafting
and flags the handful that need one confirmation click before publication. Nothing
in `bibliography.bib` or the article is invented; where I could not independently
confirm a pinpoint, I say so rather than guessing.

**Standard caveat.** I verified *existence and core holding/text* against
authoritative public sources (Justia, CourtListener, FindLaw, Cornell LII for
cases; the Nebraska Legislature's official site and the Nebraska DNR GWMPA
compilation for statutes; UNL Digital Commons / National Ag Law Center for the
secondary literature). Before you publish, you should still (a) confirm pinpoint
page cites against Westlaw/Lexis, and (b) confirm the **current** statutory text —
§ 46-739 and related sections were amended as recently as **Laws 2025, LB317**, so
subsection lettering may have shifted from what the reissue shows.

## One thing to eyeball on your first Quarto render

The only real render risk is the **biblatex → CSL mapping for the `@jurisdiction`
(case) and `@legislation` (statute) entries**. Pandoc's citeproc maps
`@jurisdiction → legal_case` and `@legislation → legislation`, and I cited
Nebraska cases to the **official "Neb." reporter** so the court is implied and the
Epps CSL should render, e.g., `Olson v. City of Wahoo, 124 Neb. 802 (1933)` with no
court field. If your first render drops the reporter or the year on a case cite,
the fix is a two-minute one and matches **Prof. Epps's own documented workflow**:
enter cases (and case short forms) **manually in the footnote text** rather than
through the managed pipeline. The article already types all **statutes** by hand in
the footnotes for exactly this reason (the Epps style documents "limited
statute/regulation coverage"), so only the case cites are pipeline-rendered.

If you prefer strict law-review **regional-reporter** form (`248 N.W. 304 (Neb.
1933)`) over the official-reporter form, swap each case entry's `journaltitle` to
`N.W.`/`N.W.2d`, put the N.W. volume/first-page in `volume`/`pages` (the parallels
are in each entry's `note` field), and add the court — or just hand-type them.
